# Survey 2 Knowledge Base — How the TA Discovers and Evaluates Products Online

## Purpose
This document consolidates Survey 2 into one reusable project knowledge base.

It explains:
- where the TA spends attention online
- which platforms are most embedded in everyday life
- which platforms are most trusted
- which platforms are most tolerated as ad environments
- what formats the TA consumes on each platform
- what topics dominate the TA's attention
- how the TA uses communities, creators, search, and AI tools
- how AI is beginning to shape product discovery and purchase decisions

This KB is intended to support:
- TA persona updates
- Discovery Ads semantic-core updates
- channel strategy
- creative strategy
- landing-page trust architecture
- creator / adjacency planning
- AI-search / AI-readable content planning

---

## 1. Executive summary

### Core truth
The TA is a **digitally active, news-aware, utility-seeking, and increasingly AI-comfortable audience**.

This is not a light-media or purely entertainment-first audience.  
Their online behavior is shaped by three strong forces:

1. **high everyday digital usage**
2. **high public-affairs / news attention**
3. **strong practical-utility behavior** in search, research, reviews, planning, and decision support

### Most important strategic conclusion
For this TA, digital discovery works best when it respects the difference between:

- **reach environments**
- **trust environments**
- **evaluation environments**
- **community environments**
- **AI-assisted decision environments**

These are not the same thing.

### Highest-level media picture
The TA's digital ecosystem breaks down like this:

- **Facebook, YouTube, and online news** are the most embedded broad-reach everyday environments
- **Online news, Reddit, and YouTube** are the strongest trust / evaluation environments
- **Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok** behave more like passive feed environments
- **Reddit and online news** are more ad-tolerant than many marketers would assume
- **Google** is still the overwhelming search default
- **ChatGPT-led AI use** is already mainstream in the TA and is beginning to influence real purchase decisions

## 2. The TA's digital habitat

## Everyday platform presence
The TA is a **high-digital-usage audience**, not a digitally light audience.

The strongest everyday environments are:
- **Facebook**
- **Online news sites**
- **YouTube**

These three combine the highest daily usage and the strongest weekly reach.

### Secondary but meaningful platforms
- **Instagram**
- **Reddit**

Both matter, but they are more selective and behaviorally distinct than the top three.

### Polarized / overestimatable platforms
- **X**
- **TikTok**

Both have meaningful users, but also large non-user groups.  
They should not be treated as universal TA channels.

## Forced-choice priority
When forced to keep only one platform for news and content, the TA most often chooses:
1. **Online news sites**
2. **Facebook**
3. **YouTube**

This is important because it shows that under constraint, the TA prioritizes:
- information utility
- familiarity
- broad usefulness
more than pure social entertainment.

## 3. Platform roles in the TA's mind

## Intent-rich / evaluation platforms
The strongest platforms for active search, full consumption, and deeper evaluation are:
- **Online news sites**
- **YouTube**
- **Reddit**

These behave like:
- research spaces
- evaluation spaces
- full-attention environments
- deeper consideration environments

## Feed-driven / passive discovery platforms
The strongest passive / skim-driven environments are:
- **Facebook**
- **Instagram**
- **TikTok**

These behave more like:
- awareness environments
- casual discovery environments
- emotional-hook environments
- repeated-light-exposure environments

## Discussion-sensitive environments
The strongest discussion or community platforms are:
- **Reddit**
- **X**
- to a lesser extent **Facebook**

These are especially relevant for:
- social proof
- peer validation
- scrutiny
- opinion formation
- comment-led persuasion or rejection

## 4. Trust and ad comfort by platform

## Strongest information-trust environments
The TA trusts:
1. **Online news sites**
2. **Reddit**
3. **YouTube**

### Meaning
This is one of the most important results in the survey.

It means:
- broad usage does not equal trust
- trust is concentrated in environments that feel more informative, deliberate, and explanation-friendly
- credibility and reach should be planned separately

## High-reach but lower-trust environments
- **Facebook**
- **Instagram**
- **TikTok**

These can generate attention, but they are weaker as pure reassurance environments.

## Strongest ad-tolerance environments
The TA tolerates ads best on:
- **Reddit**
- **Online news sites**
- then a middle tier including **Instagram** and **X**

### Most irritating ad environments
- **YouTube**
- **Facebook**

This matters because some of the most-used platforms are not the most comfortable ad environments.

### Key conclusion
A platform can be:
- widely used
- even trusted to some degree
- but still disliked as an ad environment

This is especially important for YouTube.

## 5. Platform-format fit

## Dominant format by platform
The TA's format expectations are very platform-specific:

- **YouTube** = longer videos / in-depth reviews
- **Facebook** = short text posts
- **Instagram** = short videos
- **Reddit** = user discussions / comments
- **X** = short text posts
- **TikTok** = short videos
- **Online news** = longer written articles

## Strategic meaning
This means "digital content" cannot be treated as one thing.

For the TA:
- **YouTube** is where explanation belongs
- **Online news** is where mature written credibility belongs
- **Facebook** is where concise copy-led framing works best
- **Instagram / TikTok** are where compressed visual hooks belong
- **Reddit** is where the message must survive discussion

## 6. What the TA pays attention to online

## Broad attention map
The TA's online attention is led first by:
1. **Politics / current events / news**
2. **Sports**
3. **Entertainment / pop culture**

Then comes a strong practical-utility layer:
- finance
- health / fitness
- technology / science
- DIY / how-to / reviews
- food / cooking

## Single strongest topic
When forced to choose one topic followed most closely, the TA most often selects:
- **Politics / public affairs**
followed by:
- **Sports**
- **News / current events**

### Meaning
The TA is not only casually exposed to politics and news.  
For a large share, public-affairs awareness is the **top priority attention layer**.

## Key attention identity
The TA is best described as:
- **news-aware**
- **current-events aware**
- **utility-seeking**
- **adult-interest oriented**
- **not purely entertainment-led**

This is a very important strategic signal for messaging tone.

## 7. Communities, subscriptions, and creator ecosystems

## Active-follow topics
When the TA actively follows communities, subscriptions, newsletters, channels, or forums, the strongest territories are:
- **Politics / news / public affairs**
- **Sports**
- **Health / fitness**
- **Food / cooking**
- **Finance**
- **Travel**
- **Hobbies / crafts**
- **Pets / animals**

### Meaning
The community layer is more:
- intentional
- recurring
- identity-linked
- utility-oriented
than the general browsing layer.

## Specific creators / channels
Exact follows are highly fragmented.  
There is no single dominant creator universe.

Instead, the TA clusters into recurring ecosystems:
- news / politics commentary
- sports creators and sports media
- food / cooking creators
- travel / Disney creators
- health / fitness creators
- finance / investing creators
- Reddit communities
- hobby / craft groups

### Meaning
The TA is better understood through:
- **creator ecosystems**
- **topic communities**
- **adjacency types**
than through one or two universal creator names

## 8. Search behavior

## Search default
The TA is overwhelmingly:
- **Google-first**

AI search tools are present, but still clearly secondary.

### Search stack
- **Google** dominates
- **ChatGPT / AI search tools** form the strongest secondary layer
- Bing, DuckDuckGo, Yahoo, and Grok are niche

### Meaning
The TA may discover through many platforms, but when uncertainty appears, they still resolve it through familiar search behavior.

## 9. AI adoption and AI-assisted decision behavior

## AI tool adoption
This is **not** an AI-resistant audience.

The strongest AI pattern is:
- **ChatGPT** is the default assistant
- **Google Gemini** is the strongest secondary tool
- **Microsoft Copilot** is the third layer
- **Claude** is meaningful but smaller
- many respondents use more than one assistant

### Meaning
The TA is:
- AI-aware
- already comfortable with assistants
- increasingly multi-tool
- not fully universal in adoption, but clearly beyond experimentation

## AI use cases
The strongest everyday AI jobs are:
- quick questions and explanations
- writing help
- work-related help
- ideas and inspiration
- shopping research
- summaries
- planning
- tech help

### Meaning
AI is primarily a **friction-reduction utility layer**, not an entertainment novelty.

It helps the TA:
- save time
- compare options
- generate ideas
- plan tasks
- process information
- write more efficiently
- solve problems

## Comfort with AI for guidance
Most of the TA is already comfortable using AI:
- across a range of topics
- and a meaningful group relies on it often

Only a small minority avoids AI or has not tried it.

### Meaning
The TA is better described as:
- **AI-comfortable**
than:
- AI-skeptical

## AI influence on buying
This is one of the most important findings:

- **40%** say AI has already influenced something they decided to buy
- **37%** say it has not yet, but could in the future

### Meaning
AI has already entered the TA's purchase-decision workflow for a meaningful share of respondents, and future openness is larger than outright rejection.

## 10. Unified model of how the TA discovers and evaluates products online

Across Survey 2, the TA appears to follow this pattern:

### Step 1: Passive discovery happens in feed environments
Primary environments:
- Facebook
- Instagram
- TikTok
- sometimes YouTube

This is where:
- first awareness
- light curiosity
- repeated visibility
- emotional hooks
often happen

### Step 2: Stronger attention shifts into intent-rich environments
Primary environments:
- YouTube
- Online news
- Reddit
- Google search

This is where:
- explanation
- reviews
- product comparisons
- detail-gathering
- trust formation
usually happen

### Step 3: Community and creator ecosystems deepen relevance
Primary environments:
- Reddit communities
- sports / news / food / travel / hobby creator ecosystems
- topic subscriptions and newsletters

This is where:
- repeated relevance
- niche trust
- peer validation
- habit formation
can happen

### Step 4: AI increasingly enters the decision workflow
Primary behaviors:
- quick questions
- comparisons
- shopping research
- summaries
- writing / planning
- guidance across many everyday tasks

This means discovery and evaluation are no longer only human-platform-driven.  
They are increasingly **AI-mediated** as well.

## 12. Final synthesis

Survey 2 shows a TA that is digitally active, current-events aware, utility-seeking, and increasingly AI-comfortable. Their everyday media life is concentrated in Facebook, YouTube, and online news, but their trust and evaluation behavior shifts more strongly into online news, Reddit, YouTube, and Google. They do not use all platforms the same way: some are for passive discovery, some for deep evaluation, some for discussion, and some for habitual community return. Their topic attention is led by politics, news, and sports, with a strong secondary layer of finance, health, technology, DIY, food, and other practical-interest territories. AI is no longer peripheral: it is already a multi-purpose everyday utility layer and is beginning to influence real purchase decisions. For ECHO, the winning strategic lesson is clear: discovery must be planned as a system that separates reach, trust, evaluation, community, and AI-mediated decision behavior.
