# TA’s Gaming Habits Knowledge Base

Structured synthesis of the gaming-habits survey for ECHO positioning, creative development, and future project work.

## How to use this document
Treat this as the current reference point for what the target audience actually plays, remembers, recognizes, and worries about. Use it to keep positioning, messaging, design choices, and game references grounded in survey evidence rather than assumptions.

**Method note:** Several source files are row-level semantic splits. Where one respondent mentioned multiple ideas, the answer was split into multiple mentions. Counts in those sections are therefore mention counts, not always mutually exclusive respondents.

## 1. Executive summary
- This TA plays often. 81% report playing games daily or several times a week, and 94% have played on an arcade machine at least a few times.
- The emotional core is nostalgic, but not narrow. The strongest memory zones are 8-bit / 16-bit consoles, Atari-era home systems, and early arcade classics.
- Social play is mostly non-video. Across friends, partners, and children/grandchildren, board games and card games dominate, with video games acting as a supporting layer rather than the whole story.
- Classic arcade memory is highly concentrated. Pac-Man is the dominant anchor by a wide margin, followed by Donkey Kong, Galaga, Space Invaders, Frogger, Mortal Kombat, Street Fighter, Centipede, and Asteroids.
- Home-arcade purchase friction is practical, not ideological: price, space, maintenance, fear of low use, and reliability are the main barriers.
- When ECHO is shown with the screen off, many respondents either misclassify it or cannot infer the game experience. The screen, recognizable gameplay, and social setting must do the explanatory work.

## 2. At-a-glance audience profile
- **How often they play:** 51% daily; 30% several times a week
- **Arcade exposure:** 71% played on arcade machines many times; 23% a few times
- **Nostalgia zone:** 33% 8-bit / 16-bit consoles; 27% Atari / early home consoles; 26% early arcade classics
- **Preferred single game type:** Mobile (24%), console video games (19%), board games (18%), PC games (13%), card games (8%)
- **Likely home placement:** Basement (30%) or dedicated game room (29%)

## 3. Core gaming profile of the TA
The TA is not best described as a hardcore gamer, a casual mobile-only gamer, or an arcade purist. They are a broad, experienced, memory-rich gaming audience. Their habits combine classic arcade awareness, home-console nostalgia, mainstream household game-night behavior, and enough modern gaming literacy to recognize or play Fortnite, Mario Kart, Roblox, Minecraft, Call of Duty, and similar titles.

Key signals:
- **High play frequency:** gaming is a routine part of life, not a rare hobby.
- **Broad platform familiarity:** mobile, console, board, PC, card, and arcade all appear in the survey.
- **Very high arcade familiarity:** arcade references land; the category itself does not need explanation.
- **Strong nostalgia bias:** recognition and memory are the strongest emotional levers.

## 4. What they worry about before buying a home arcade
Top concern buckets:
- Cost / price / investment — 29
- Space / size / placement — 26
- Maintenance / repairs / parts — 19
- Low usage / practicality — 10
- Reliability / longevity — 6
- Limited games / replayability — 3

**Plain-language read:** the audience is not rejecting the idea of a home arcade. They are testing whether the machine is worth the money, fits the house, will last, and will actually get used.

## 7. Social play behavior by context
### Friends
- Dominant mix: board games + card games + party games
- Top named games: Poker, Cards Against Humanity, Monopoly, Mario Kart, Catan
- Medium split: 74% non-video / 26% video

### Partner / spouse
- Dominant mix: board games + card games + word / trivia play
- Top named games: Scrabble, Uno, Connect Four, Rummy, Catan
- Medium split: 79% non-video / 21% video

### Children / grandchildren
- Dominant mix: board games first, then card games, then family-friendly video games
- Top named games: Monopoly, Uno, Mario Kart, Checkers, Candy Land
- Medium split: 69% non-video / 31% video

**Interpretation:** togetherness for this TA usually means familiar, low-friction, easy-to-explain play. That supports ECHO as a social centerpiece and shared ritual, not as a high-skill gamer device.

## 12. Working model of the TA’s gaming habits
- They are broad, experienced gamers with both nostalgia and current literacy.
- They instinctively trust familiar names more than abstract genre claims.
- They think of social play mainly through board games, card games, and easy household competition.
- Their favorite-game memory is larger than arcade alone; Nintendo, tabletop, RPG, and classic-console signals all matter.
- Their arcade memory, however, is extremely stable: Pac-Man and the classic cabinet canon are the safest recognition anchors.
- For a home arcade, the biggest emotional opportunity is nostalgia and shared play; the biggest practical barrier is price + space + maintenance + fear of low use.
- The machine must look like it belongs in a finished adult home environment, not just in a game-store fantasy or a child-only playroom.
