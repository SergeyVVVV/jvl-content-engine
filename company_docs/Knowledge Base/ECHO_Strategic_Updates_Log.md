## 2026-04-14 - Meta Ads Text Performance Analysis

### 6. First campaign confirmation that the KB's emotion-first persuasion sequence produces the most efficient Discovery clicks

The Easter campaign (March 15 - April 13, 2026) ran four distinct text variants across identical targeting and placements. When aggregated by text:

- The text with a sharp emotional opener followed by a fast pivot to category + proof + CTA (the Gift text) produced the lowest CPC ($0.18) and second-highest CTR (2.62%).
- The text that opened with warranty/safety language (proof-first) produced the highest CPC ($0.22) and second-lowest CTR (2.06%).
- The text that stacked multiple routes in one ad (nostalgia + bar culture + social + solo + ease) produced the highest raw CTR (3.36%) but at the highest CPM ($6.49), suggesting it reached a responsive but narrower and more expensive audience.

The proven text structure is: sharp 1-2 line emotional hook (one idea only), then category + proof point, then ease bridge, then CTA.

**What it changes:**
- The "emotion first, proof second" sequence is now a campaign-confirmed operating rule, not only a research recommendation.
- Proof-layer messages (warranty, support, durability) should be explicitly restricted to retargeting and bottom-funnel environments - not used as Discovery openers.
- Multi-route stacking in a single text (nostalgia + bar + social + solo + ease in one ad) is a known efficiency risk: it can generate volume but at higher cost and with less audience precision.
- The proven text structure (emotional hook, category + proof, ease bridge, CTA) should be documented as the current best-performing Discovery text template.

**Files affected:**
- ECHO_Execution_Playbook_Evidence_Based.md (Section 3 - add campaign-confirmed status; Section 4 - restrict warranty openers from Discovery)
- ECHO_Discovery_Ads_Semantic_Core.md (Section 3 - strengthen Priority 5 restriction; add proven text template)
- ECHO_Brief_Template_Pack_Evidence_Based.md (Section 5 - add proven structure reference; note warranty/proof-first is not for Discovery)
- ECHO_Meta_Ads_Call_Log.md (add strategic reference under Proactive Ideas)

## 2026-04-14 - Evergreen Ad Creative System

### 7. Evergreen ad creative framework and statistically grounded A/B testing methodology established

Three image bases were mapped to three distinct audience modes, each with natural headline pairings:

- **Bartop** (machine on counter, warm lighting) - nostalgia mode - "Poker Night Is Back" / "Deal Everyone In"
- **Gift** (gift box, flowers, neutral background) - gift-buyer mode - "A Gift That Stays" / "Give Them the Arcade Back"
- **Friends** (split frame, people + machine) - social/togetherness mode - "Friends Over - Game On" / "Who's Hosting Game Night?"

Two additional headline pairs were created for game-screen variants:
- Score chaser screen: "The Arcade Is Back" / "Who's Got the High Score?"
- Video reel: "Remember All of These?" / "A Game for Every Guest"

All ads share constant elements: "Home arcade - 149 games" as secondary line, "Bring It Home" as CTA. Supporting body text follows the proven structure from Entry 6.

A sequential isolation A/B testing methodology was established using Easter campaign CPM data: 0.5pp minimum detectable effect, 95% confidence, 80% power, requiring ~16,770 impressions per ad. Each ad runs in its own ad set with equal daily budget to prevent premature algorithmic bias. Kill rule: 2x CPC gap after threshold impressions. Four rounds test one variable at a time: concept - message - game screen - format.

**What it changes:**
- Ad creative production now follows a systematic concept-to-audience-mode mapping rather than ad-hoc creative generation.
- A/B testing across all paid channels should use the same statistical framework, adjusted for platform-specific delivery rates.
- Secondary line and CTA are locked as constants unless a future test specifically isolates them.
- Game screen selection follows a data-backed hierarchy: poker (proven by campaign data + TA survey), score chaser (maps to TA's strongest arcade-memory zone), video reel (tests breadth-of-value).

**Files affected:**
- ECHO_Discovery_Ads_Semantic_Core.md (add concept-paired headlines to approved bank, add testing methodology reference)
- ECHO_Brief_Template_Pack_Evidence_Based.md (add testing methodology as a reusable framework section)
- ECHO_Meta_Ads_Call_Log.md (reference under Proactive Ideas)

## 2026-04-20 - ECHO Website Audience Architecture

### 9. B2C / B2B page separation confirmed as the website audience architecture

The ECHO web presence will split into two dedicated pages instead of a single multi-audience product page:

- **Echo for Home** - primary B2C page, aimed at home/individual buyers.
- **Echo for Business** - combined page for SMB operators and distributors/resellers.

Content for one audience will not appear on the other. Product variants (coin-acceptor vs. non-coin-acceptor) remain referenceable on both pages as optional configurations, but the primary narrative of each page is audience-specific. This decision resolves an open tension in older briefs where B2B credibility signals were informally appearing on consumer-facing materials.

**What it changes:**
- All future paid traffic must be routed to the audience-appropriate page. Home-targeted ads land on Echo for Home; operator/distributor-targeted outreach lands on Echo for Business.
- Assets, ads, and outreach pieces for home buyers must not surface B2B value propositions (revenue potential, investment framing), and vice versa.
- The Amazon consumer-first listing remains the B2C anchor. The Echo for Business page becomes the landing destination for distributor/reseller outreach and for the commercial/coin-operated version of the product.
- Brief templates must specify which page the asset targets so copy and proof selection align with the correct audience.
- The longer-term distribution-wind-down strategy should shape how much architectural weight the Echo for Business page gives to distributor/reseller content versus SMB direct-operator content (worth a deliberate design decision before the page is built, not after).

**Files affected:**
- ECHO_Execution_Playbook_Evidence_Based.md (Section 6 - add audience-split rule to landing page guidance)
- Unified_TA_Master_KB.md (note that B2B is a separate addressable audience, not a trust proof for B2C)
- ECHO_Brief_Template_Pack_Evidence_Based.md (brief inputs should specify which page the asset targets)
