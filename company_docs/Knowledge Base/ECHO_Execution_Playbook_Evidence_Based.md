# ECHO Execution Playbook - Evidence-Based

Working execution manual for ad managers, designers, landing-page owners, and marketplace teams.
Built from the Unified TA Master KB, the updated TA Persona, the premium online shopping KB, and the Discovery Ads Semantic Core.

## 1. What this document is for
Use this playbook when you need to turn the current project reference into execution decisions.
It is not another research narrative. It is the operating layer that answers:
- What do we now believe about the TA?
- Why do we believe it?
- How confident are we?
- What does that change in ads, design, landing pages, Amazon, and support?

## 2. Non-negotiable TA truths - belief / evidence / confidence / implication
### 1. Nostalgia is the strongest first-interest trigger.
- Evidence source - Survey 3 message test; persona and unified KB.
- Confidence - High
- Execution implication - Ads should usually open with classics / nostalgia before shifting to proof. Designers should support this with credible retro cues, not childish styling. Landing pages should carry nostalgia in the headline but close with proof.

### 2. Togetherness is the strongest secondary route and often beats premium language.
- Evidence source - Survey 3 message test; image-only reason-to-buy results.
- Confidence - High
- Execution implication - Use family / friends / shared-play frames as a major alternate route. Show believable social use. On-page copy should translate nostalgia into real shared-home moments.

### 3. The TA is open to new products online, but not to uncertain ownership.
- Evidence source - Survey 3 premium shopping KB and blocker analysis.
- Confidence - High
- Execution implication - Do not over-focus on familiarity barriers. Focus on reducing proof gaps: reviews, quality, support, value, warranty, and fit-in-home reassurance.

### 4. Amazon is the default shopping home base, but trust needs rise fast when the purchase feels premium, technical, or expensive.
- Evidence source - Survey 3 Amazon-first and Amazon-exception findings.
- Confidence - High
- Execution implication - Use Amazon as a strong habit-aligned route, but do not assume Amazon alone solves trust. Direct-site and listing content both need a heavy proof layer.

### 5. The biggest conversion blockers are weak reviews, weak review volume, unjustified price, and low confidence in durability / support.
- Evidence source - Survey 3 purchase blockers and unified KB.
- Confidence - High
- Execution implication - Ads must not promise abstract premium without justification. Designers must make proof visible. Landing pages and listings need reviews, close-ups, written detail, warranty / return clarity, and value articulation.

### 6. The product is naturally read from image alone as a gaming device and family-play object, not as a clearly defined premium home arcade.
- Evidence source - Survey 3 image-only search language and main-reason results.
- Confidence - High
- Execution implication - Use explicit category anchors in ads and pages - home arcade, arcade machine for home, touchscreen arcade. Premium adult-home meaning must be added through copy, environment, and proof.

### 7. The most persuasive visual proof is detail close-ups, scale-in-room proof, real-customer media, and demo-style function video.
- Evidence source - Survey 3 visual priority and single-best-first-visual tests.
- Confidence - High
- Execution implication - Hero assets should strongly consider demo video or credible in-use proof. Designers should prioritize close-ups, room scale, and real ownership realism over glossy lifestyle-first presentation.

### 8. The TA usually decides in a few days, not instantly and not after a very long cycle.
- Evidence source - Survey 3 decision-time findings.
- Confidence - Medium-high
- Execution implication - Build for a short-consideration window: immediate clarity, then remarketing and repeat proof. Urgency can support, but reassurance matters more than pressure.

### 9. The TA is digitally active and broad-interest, with strong reach in Facebook, YouTube, and online news, and stronger trust / evaluation behavior in YouTube, online news, Reddit, and Google-led research.
- Evidence source - Survey 2 discovery / evaluation KB and persona.
- Confidence - High
- Execution implication - Creative should separate reach from evaluation. Reach ads can open emotion. Evaluation content should add explanation, reviews, comparisons, and proof. Search and AI-readable clarity matter.

### 10. The buyer is quietly premium, grounded, and ownership-focused - not a luxury-for-status seeker and not a gamer-identity buyer.
- Evidence source - Unified KB, persona update, earlier persona correction.
- Confidence - High
- Execution implication - Avoid flashy luxury, hardcore gamer language, or novelty-tech energy. Use warm, mature, restrained language and adult-home visual context.

### 11. Family/shared-home use is the primary natural interpretation; solo enjoyment and gifting are meaningful secondary routes.
- Evidence source - Survey 3 image-only reason-to-buy analysis.
- Confidence - Medium-high
- Execution implication - Family route should be a default option in briefs. Solo decompression and gifting can be used as secondary audience variants. Commercial and bar routes should be treated as narrower segments.

### 12. Live chat is the most convenient seller-contact method, with email second and phone third.
- Evidence source - Survey 3 communication preference findings; persona.
- Confidence - Medium-high
- Execution implication - Direct-site support should make live chat visible, keep email clear, and use phone as reassurance / escalation rather than the main convenience path.

## 3. TA persuasion model / message hierarchy
The working rule is simple - emotion opens, proof closes. The buyer is open to ECHO, but only buys confidently when interest is followed by strong verification and ownership reassurance.

**Campaign-confirmed (April 2026):** The Easter Meta Ads campaign validated this sequence with real performance data. The text that followed this model most closely (sharp emotional hook into category + proof + CTA) produced the lowest CPC ($0.18) and strong CTR (2.62%). The text that inverted the sequence by opening with warranty/safety language produced the highest CPC ($0.22) and low CTR (2.06%). This is now an operating rule, not only a research recommendation.

| Priority | Route | Role | What it sounds like | How to use it |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Nostalgia | Default opening route | Bring back the classics. Relive arcade nostalgia in a modern home-ready format. | Use as the broadest first-touch route. |
| 2 | Togetherness | Best secondary route | Connect across generations. Turn hanging out into playing together. | Use for family, hosting, and shared-home variants. |
| 3 | Ease / ownership | Bridge from emotion to conversion | Plug & play. Easy to start, easy to come back to, easy to own. | Use after the emotional hook or for friction-sensitive buyers. |
| 4 | Curated library value | Rational support route | All the favorites in one place. No hunting, no setup hassle. | Good support route for listings, LP subheads, and retargeting. |
| 5 | Adult-home fit / quiet premium | Selective late-stage route | Looks right in a real adult home and feels worth owning for years. | Use narrowly and only with proof. |

### Game-library language rule
All 149 games are JVL's own proprietary titles. No licensed third-party game names exist in the library. Sell the library by gameplay style and visual experience, not by title recognition. Language like "favorites" or "classics" should refer to the style of play (maze chase, card games, score chasers), not imply licensed brands. Card game names (poker, blackjack, solitaire) are generic and safe to use. Do not reference trademarked game titles (Pac-Man, Donkey Kong, Galaga, etc.) anywhere, even in descriptive form.

### Core persuasion sequence
- Open with nostalgia or togetherness.
- Clarify the category quickly - home arcade, arcade machine for home, touchscreen arcade for home.
- Bridge to low-friction ownership - plug & play, all-in-one, easy to come back to.
- Close with proof - reviews, detail shots, room scale, warranty / return clarity, support, and value justification.

### Proven Discovery text structure (campaign-confirmed April 2026)
The strongest-performing Discovery ad text followed this template:
1. **Line 1-2:** One sharp emotional hook. One idea only (nostalgia or togetherness). Concrete and visual enough to stop the scroll.
2. **Line 3:** Category clarity + one proof point. Name what ECHO is (home arcade) and include library value or a specific feature.
3. **Line 4:** Ease bridge. Plug and play, no setup, one machine.
4. **Line 5:** Short CTA.

### What does not work as a Discovery opener
- Warranty, support, and durability language. These are strong trust signals but belong in retargeting and bottom-funnel environments, not as first-touch Discovery openers. Campaign data confirmed this: warranty-first text produced the worst CPC and low CTR.
- Multi-route stacking. Combining nostalgia + bar culture + social + solo + ease in one text can generate click volume, but at higher CPM and with less audience precision. One driver per text.

## 4. Ad-manager playbook
### 4.1 Core rules
- One asset / one message route. Do not stack nostalgia + family + ease + premium in one headline.
- Default opening order: nostalgia first, togetherness second, ease third, library fourth.
- Use explicit category anchors with the image: home arcade, arcade machine for home, touchscreen arcade for home.
- Treat premium language as support, not the broadest opener.
- Translate price through ownership value: years of use, shared moments, all-in-one convenience, durability, and fit in the home.
- Assume the buyer may click with emotion but will only convert with proof.

### 4.2 Hard do-not-do list
- Do not lead with luxury-for-status language.
- Do not use gamer jargon, esports energy, or console-culture framing.
- Do not make the product feel like a generic monitor, tablet, or ambiguous gaming gadget.
- Do not rely on specs as the first layer of persuasion.
- Do not use kiddie, novelty-tech, or clutter-like tone.
- Do not use warranty, support, or durability language as a Discovery opener. These are proof-layer signals for retargeting and bottom-funnel only. (Campaign-confirmed April 2026.)
- Do not stack multiple message routes in a single text (e.g., nostalgia + bar culture + social + solo + ease). One driver per text. (Campaign-confirmed April 2026.)

### 4.3 Copy formulas that fit the current TA
| Formula type | Working structure |
|---|---|

| Togetherness opener | A fun way to connect across generations - [family / friends / guests line]. |

| Library support | [Curated / all-in-one] game library - [favorite / classic / built-in proof]. |

### 4.4 Best route by objective
| Objective | Best route | Backup route | Notes |

## 5. Designer / creative playbook
### 5.1 Visual priority stack
- Demo-style video showing how it works.
- Close-ups of controls, materials, build quality, and finish.
- Size / scale in room - show how it fits on real surfaces and in real spaces.
- Real-customer or believable in-use proof.
- Room-setting images in adult-home environments.
- Annotated graphics only after the object is visually understood.

### 5.2 What to avoid
- Do not lead with pure lifestyle gloss or status-heavy premium scenes.
- Do not let the product float without clear scale.
- Do not crop away controls, screen context, or physical form so much that the object becomes generic.
- Do not over-stylize the room so the product feels like decor first and category second.

### 5.3 Required visual proof stack for premium trust
- One strong function proof asset - preferably demo video or credible in-use proof.
- One scale proof asset - clearly shows size in room / on furniture.
- One detail proof asset - controls, screen, finish, materials, build quality.
- One social proof asset - real-customer or believable ownership realism.
- One adult-home context asset - warm, quiet, mature room styling.
- One origin / heritage proof asset - "Designed in Canada, made in the USA" and/or "Built on 30+ years of JVL bartop expertise."

### 5.5 Thin-review proof-burden note
ECHO currently has very thin review coverage (9 reviews, 4.3 average, only 3 verified with text or images as of April 2026). Until the review base is substantially stronger, every conversion environment - listing, landing page, retargeting - must carry a heavier-than-normal proof burden. Visual proof, written detail, warranty clarity, and origin/heritage signals must compensate for what a strong review base would normally provide on its own.

### 5.4 Environment and styling rules
- Rooms should feel adult, lived-in, and tasteful - not flashy luxury and not childish playroom energy.
- Use warm, believable home lighting and practical room context.
- Keep the object legible. Do not let props overpower the category read.
- When people are shown, the use should feel natural and low-theater - casual family, couple, or guest interaction rather than staged excitement.

## 6. Landing-page, Amazon, and support playbook
- Hero section should combine the strongest opening route with explicit category clarity.
- The first trust stack should appear quickly: reviews, review volume, clear photos, written clarity, and visible support / policy cues.
- Use proof-heavy sequencing: hero -> what it is -> why it is worth it -> how it fits the home -> what people say -> ownership reassurance.
- Product pages should emphasize written detail, what is included, warranty / return clarity, and realistic ownership benefits.
- Keep live chat visible, email easy to find, and phone available for reassurance. Note: this applies to direct-site and off-Amazon environments only. Amazon does not allow seller contact details on listings.
- Amazon listings should work harder on verification because premium trust is not automatic on Amazon.

### 6.1 Suggested page order
- Hero - nostalgia or togetherness plus explicit category anchor.
- Fast object clarity - what it is and where it fits in the home.
- Why it is worth it - all-in-one value, ease, and ownership payoff.
- Visual proof - demo, close-ups, room scale, real-customer proof.
- Written proof - detailed description, what is in the box, game-library framing, practical use cases.
- Trust layer - reviews, warranty, return policy, support, contact options.
- Conversion layer - clear CTA plus reassurance directly nearby.

### 6.2 Amazon-specific rule
Amazon is habit-aligned, but premium trust is conditional. Listing content must reduce verification risk, not only present convenience. Treat reviews, detail images, size proof, and support / warranty clarity as core conversion content, not extras.

### 6.3 Amazon contact restriction
Amazon does not allow seller contact information (email, phone, URL) in A+ content or anywhere on the product listing. Seller contact visibility is only possible through the seller profile page or Amazon Brand Store. Do not plan contact placement on the listing itself.

### 6.4 Available proof points for listings and pages
In addition to reviews, close-ups, and policy clarity, the following confirmed proof points should be used in proof stacks where relevant:
- "Designed in Canada, made in the USA" - concrete origin trust signal.
- "ECHO is built on 30+ years of JVL bartop expertise. JVL bartops have been awarded 'Best Bartop Video Game of the Year' by RePlay Magazine multiple times." - heritage framing. Do not claim the award for ECHO directly. Do not use the RePlay Magazine logo without separate approval.

## 7. Fast QA checklists
### 7.1 Ads
- Is the route clear - nostalgia, togetherness, ease, library, or adult-home fit?
- Is category language explicit enough to prevent generic gaming-device confusion?
- Does the line sound warm, mature, and grounded?
- Is premium language justified rather than decorative?
- Would the asset still make sense if the viewer sees only the image and one short line?

### 7.2 Design / creative
- Can the viewer tell what the product is within one glance?
- Is there enough scale proof?
- Is there enough detail proof?
- Does the visual feel adult-home and believable rather than glossy or gimmicky?
- Would this asset still help if the buyer is trying to verify quality, fit, and ownership confidence?

### 7.3 Landing page / listing / support
- Are reviews and review credibility easy to see?
- Are return, warranty, and support paths clear?
- Is value justification explicit?
- Is there enough written detail to reduce uncertainty?
- Is the next-step contact path obvious and low-friction?

## 8. Final working rule
Best shorthand for execution:

- Open with nostalgia or togetherness.
- Clarify the category fast.
- Make it feel like it belongs in a real adult home.
- Close with reviews, proof, value clarity, durability confidence, and visible support.

That is the evidence-based route most likely to fit the current TA.
