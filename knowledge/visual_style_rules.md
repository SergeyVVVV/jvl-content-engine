# visual_style_rules

## Purpose
This file defines the working visual style rules for JVL ECHO.
Use it for ad creative, landing pages, Amazon assets, product-page imagery, motion assets, and creative briefs.

## Core visual principle
ECHO should look like a real adult-home leisure product that is emotionally inviting, clearly understood, quietly premium, and easy to trust.

The visual system should:
- open with nostalgia or togetherness
- clarify the category quickly
- show function and ownership proof early
- make the product feel believable in a real adult home
- avoid both gamer-clutter energy and flashy luxury energy

## Why this matters
The current product image is often read too generically - as a gaming device, monitor, screen, or family-play object rather than clearly as a premium home arcade.
Because of that, visuals must actively improve category clarity, proof, and adult-home meaning.

## Visual priority stack
Use this order for creative decisions whenever possible:

1. Demo-style function proof
- Show the machine working
- Show taps, gameplay motion, and immediate understandability

2. Detail close-ups
- Controls
- Screen clarity
- Materials
- Finish
- Build quality

3. Size / scale proof
- Show how it fits on real furniture and in real rooms
- Make proportions obvious

4. Real-customer or believable in-use proof
- Credible ownership realism
- Natural use, not overacted performance

5. Adult-home context
- Quiet, warm, mature environments
- Product belongs in the room, not just in a showroom

## First visual rule
If the asset is static, the visual or headline must make the category obvious.
Do not rely on silhouette alone.
When needed, pair visuals with clear category labels such as:
- home arcade
- bartop arcade
- touchscreen arcade for home
- arcade machine for home
- all-in-one arcade

## Environment rules
Preferred settings:
- den
- basement lounge
- home bar
- game room
- cabin
- lake-house style leisure space

The room should feel:
- adult
- lived-in
- tasteful
- warm
- believable
- comfortable

## Lighting and mood
Use:
- warm ambient light
- believable home lighting
- moderate contrast
- premium restraint

Avoid:
- harsh showroom lighting
- over-dramatic theatrical lighting
- childish arcade-neon overload
- over-stylized glow that makes the product feel gimmicky

## What to show
### Always helpful
- the machine clearly visible
- readable product shape
- real scale in the room
- believable use posture
- close-ups of screen, controls, finish, and materials
- function cues that explain how it works

### Strong supporting visuals
- believable family / friends / guests scenes
- calm solo-use moments
- side-by-side play if relevant
- room-fit context
- real-world surfaces and furniture

## What not to show
- noisy gamer-bedroom aesthetic
- childish playroom energy
- generic tech-clutter framing
- unrealistic luxury mansion cues
- mood-only lifestyle scenes with weak product clarity
- over-polished fake reflections
- over-cropped product shots that make the object ambiguous
- gaming-neon clichés unless intentionally used in a narrow concept

## Styling rules
The product should feel:
- quietly premium
- adult-home friendly
- solid
- durable
- warm
- restrained

The product should not feel:
- flashy
- status-hungry
- toy-like
- gimmicky
- cheap
- disposable

## People in visuals
If people are shown:
- they should feel believable for the target audience
- use should feel casual and natural
- shared scenes should feel like real home interaction, not staged excitement
- expressions and body language should feel relaxed, warm, and grounded

Preferred people scenarios:
- couple at home
- family shared play
- friends / guests in a game-room or home-bar setting
- calm solo decompression scene

## Realism rules
- Preserve realistic proportions
- Preserve realistic materials
- Preserve realistic room scale
- Preserve realistic reflections and textures
- Do not over-retouch into fake perfection

## Asset recommendations by use case
### Discovery / top of funnel
Prioritize:
- nostalgia-led hero
- togetherness-led hero
- clear category read
- fast emotional hook

### Evaluation / mid funnel
Prioritize:
- demo video
- room scale
- close-up quality proof
- in-use realism

### Conversion / bottom funnel
Prioritize:
- detail close-ups
- size proof
- what's-in-the-box support visuals
- quality and finish proof
- support / warranty reassurance layers where relevant

## Final shorthand
The visual system should make ECHO feel like nostalgia made real for an adult home - clear, credible, warm, quietly premium, and easy to imagine owning for years.
