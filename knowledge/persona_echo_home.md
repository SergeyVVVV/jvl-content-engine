# ECHO Target Audience — Evidence-Based Persona

**Persona headline: The Grounded Nostalgia Buyer**

This buyer is not chasing novelty, status, or gamer identity. They want a home leisure product that feels emotionally meaningful, easy to understand, good in the home, and worth owning for years.

> "I love the idea — but it has to fit the home, feel worth the money, and still seem like a smart buy after the excitement wears off."

## At a glance

| Dimension | Evidence-based read |
|---|---|
| Life stage | Adult household, often established and home-centred |
| Core taste | Quietly premium — restrained, practical, adult |
| Digital life | Facebook / YouTube / online news heavy; Google-first; AI-comfortable |
| Shopping style | Amazon-native by habit, but proof-heavy when stakes rise |
| Gaming relationship | Broad familiarity; strong nostalgia; mainstream social play |

## Demographics

- Age: broadly 45–70 (core: 55–65)
- Life stage: established couple or household, adult or college-age children, spacious home with dedicated leisure areas
- Lifestyle: home bar, basement lounge, den, private leisure spaces; hosts friends and family; values craftsmanship and lasting purchases

## Core desires

- Reconnect with familiar game memories in an adult way
- Create easy shared moments with family, friends, or guests
- Bring something fun into the home without it feeling childish or cluttered
- Buy something that feels durable, tasteful, and satisfying to own

## Core fears

- Paying too much for something that feels gimmicky or fragile
- A machine that takes up space but never gets used
- Weak reviews, uncertain reliability, unclear support
- Something that looks good in ads but disappoints in real life

## What opens interest vs what closes the sale

| Layer | What works |
|---|---|
| Emotional openers | Nostalgia, classics, togetherness, shared memory |
| Trust builders | Reviews, real-customer proof, written clarity, support reassurance |
| Premium cues | Quiet quality, durability, adult-home fit, tasteful restraint |
| Conversion helpers | Plug & play ease, curated library, clear category labelling |

## Media and digital behaviour

| Area | Detail |
|---|---|
| Broad daily reach | Facebook, YouTube, online news |
| Stronger trust / evaluation | YouTube, online news, Reddit, Google |
| Search behaviour | Google first, AI tools second |
| Contact preference | Live chat first, then email, then phone |

## Buying behaviour

| Behaviour | Read |
|---|---|
| What opens interest | Nostalgia and cross-generational connection |
| What makes them pause | Weak reviews, unclear value, durability doubts, policy uncertainty |
| Typical decision speed | Usually a few days; sometimes 1–2 weeks |
| What they want to see first | Demo video or real-customer proof |
| What they call ECHO from image alone | "Game system", "video game", "game console" — often too generic; needs copy to anchor the category |

## ECHO use-case modes

| Mode | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Primary | Shared-home nostalgia — family play and easy social connection |
| Secondary | Solo decompressor — relaxing personal use after work or weekends |
| Secondary | Gift buyer — novelty with emotional meaning |
| Smaller segment | Guest / bar / game-room entertainer |

## Gaming and nostalgia profile

- 81% play daily or several times a week (board games, cards, casual digital)
- 94% have played arcade machines at least a few times
- Strongest nostalgia zones: 8-bit/16-bit consoles, Atari-era home systems, early arcade classics
- Social play habits: cards, board games, easy household competition
- Safest arcade reference anchors (for context only — not for use in ECHO marketing): Pac-Man, Donkey Kong, Galaga, Space Invaders, Frogger

## Trust and premium model

The TA's premium model is: Apple + Toyota + L.L.Bean + Patagonia — not flashy luxury.

- Premium = well made, dependable, tasteful, easy to live with
- Quality = durability, service, and long ownership confidence
- Bad signals = gimmicks, flimsy build, poor service, unexplained high price, loud status cues

## Messaging ladder

| Priority | Message |
|---|---|
| 1 | Bring back the classics — a modern way to relive arcade nostalgia |
| 2 | A fun way to connect across generations |
| 3 | Plug & play — set it up in minutes, enjoy it for years |
| 4 | A curated game library — all the favourites in one place |
| 5 | Premium craftsmanship and adult-home fit — use only after trust and meaning are established |

## Tone rules

### Do
- Warm, mature, grounded, and emotionally clear
- Nostalgic without sounding childish
- Premium without sounding showy
- Helpful, confident, and ownership-aware

### Do not
- Do not use gamer jargon or esports culture language
- Do not lead with luxury-for-status language
- Do not oversell specs before emotional meaning exists
- Do not make ECHO feel like novelty tech or clutter
- Do not use: "neon arcade", "ultimate beast setup", "tech-bro" tone, aggressive sales copy, childish excitement

## Visual guidance

| Visual layer | What to show |
|---|---|
| Most persuasive | Demo video, real-customer media, detail close-ups, size-in-room proof |
| Good support | Room-setting images, quiet adult-home environments, believable social use |
| Least effective first-touch | Pure lifestyle gloss, status-heavy premium scenes |
