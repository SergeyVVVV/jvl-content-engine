# brand_voice

## Purpose
This file defines the working brand voice for the JVL ECHO content engine.
Use it for briefs, landing pages, ad copy, product-page copy, marketplace content, FAQs, and supporting content.

## Core brand posture
ECHO should sound like an adult home leisure product that feels emotionally meaningful, easy to understand, quietly premium, and worth owning for years.

The brand voice should feel:
- warm
- mature
- grounded
- emotionally clear
- quietly premium
- ownership-aware
- nostalgic without sounding childish
- practical without sounding cold

## One-line brand voice summary
Warm, mature, grounded, nostalgia-led voice for an adult home leisure product - quietly premium, proof-aware, and built around safe, satisfying ownership.

## What the voice should do
- Open interest through nostalgia and togetherness
- Clarify the category quickly
- Make the product feel adult-home, not gamer-clutter
- Bridge emotion into proof, trust, and ownership confidence
- Make premium feel justified through quality, durability, support, and fit in the home

## Messaging hierarchy
Use this priority order unless the task clearly requires a narrower route:

1. Nostalgia
- Bring back the classics
- Relive arcade nostalgia in a modern home-ready format

2. Togetherness
- Connect across generations
- Create easy shared moments with family, friends, or guests

3. Ease / ownership
- Plug and play
- Easy to set up, easy to use, easy to come back to

4. Curated library value
- Familiar built-in games in one machine
- All the favorites in one place

5. Quiet premium / adult-home fit
- Made for real homes
- Built to last
- Worth owning for years

Important rule:
- Emotion opens interest. Proof closes the sale.
- Campaign-confirmed (Easter Meta Ads, April 2026): the text with a sharp emotional hook into category + proof + CTA produced the lowest CPC ($0.18). Proof-first openers (warranty, durability) produced the worst CPC ($0.22). This is an operating rule.
- Do not stack multiple message routes in one piece of copy. One driver per ad or section.

## Tone rules
### Do
- Sound like a grown-up leisure purchase, not a toy
- Keep the tone warm, restrained, and believable
- Make the product feel at home in an adult household
- Use practical reassurance when the buyer may worry about value, fit, durability, or support
- Prefer clear and useful wording over clever phrasing
- Keep premium understated and justified

### Do not
- Do not use gamer jargon
- Do not sound like esports, console culture, or gaming gear marketing
- Do not use flashy luxury-for-status language
- Do not frame ECHO as novelty tech or gadget clutter
- Do not oversell specs before emotional meaning and ownership context exist
- Do not sound childish, loud, or gimmicky

## Premium language rules
Premium for ECHO means:
- well made
- dependable
- tasteful
- mature
- restrained
- easy to live with
- worth keeping

Premium does not mean:
- flashy
- elite
- status-coded
- showy
- expensive for its own sake

## Trust and proof language
When the copy needs to move closer to conversion, emphasize:
- reviews and real customer proof
- written clarity
- quality and finish
- size / room fit
- warranty and support clarity
- ease of ownership
- value that feels earned

## Category language
Use explicit category language when clarity matters:
- home arcade
- bartop arcade
- touchscreen arcade for home
- arcade machine for home
- all-in-one arcade

Use support language when relevant:
- retro arcade for home
- 2-player arcade for home
- home bar arcade
- game room arcade
- arcade with built-in games

Avoid as primary language:
- console
- gaming equipment
- luxury
- elite
- cabinet
- novelty gadget

## Voice by funnel stage
### Top of funnel
Lead with:
- nostalgia
- familiar classics
- togetherness
- adult-home relevance

### Mid funnel
Add:
- plug and play ease
- curated game-library value
- category clarity
- home fit

### Bottom funnel
Emphasize:
- proof
- reviews
- durability confidence
- what is included
- warranty and support reassurance
- ownership confidence

## Writing style rules
- Use a short dash '-' instead of a long dash '—'
- Keep sentences clean and direct
- Prefer one strong idea per line or paragraph
- Avoid over-stacked claims
- Keep emotional language human and specific
- Keep practical language simple and useful

## Final shorthand
ECHO should sound like nostalgia made easy for a real adult home - warm, social, grounded, and worth owning.
