# SEO rules — JVL content system

## Goal of blog content

Blog content should support business growth, not traffic for its own sake. The priority is high-intent traffic from users researching a meaningful higher-ticket purchase — not casual low-value visits.

Most articles should be written for the B2C ECHO audience: affluent homeowners who value nostalgia, premium craftsmanship, and easy enjoyment at home.

## Keyword rules

- each article targets **1 primary keyword**
  - must appear in the title and slug
- up to **10 secondary keywords**
  - should appear naturally in the meta description, subheadings, body copy, and FAQ section
- use natural keyword variations — Google understands synonyms and related phrasing
  - example: "where to buy arcade machines" → "best places to buy arcade machines", "how to choose where to buy an arcade machine"
- no keyword stuffing — never repeat the same keyword in an unnatural way

## Search intent

Every article must be built around a clear search intent:

- **Informational** — user wants to learn or solve a problem
- **Commercial** — user wants to compare options, brands, or solutions
- **Transactional** — user is close to taking action (buying)

Intent matters more than search volume. The closer the intent is to JVL's product and buyer journey, the more valuable the traffic.

## Content quality rules

Each article must be:

- genuinely useful and clearly better than a generic AI-generated page
- original — unique insights, JVL-specific perspective, real product understanding
- free of fluff, filler, and vague generic statements
- structured to help the reader take a logical next step

AI should be used for outlines, ideation, and drafts — not as the final author. Human editing must add judgment, insight, clarity, and brand voice.

## What to avoid

- generic or obviously AI-written content
- repetitive or thin content
- content disconnected from JVL's real audience
- content built around wrong intent
- content written only to chase traffic with no commercial relevance
- keyword stuffing

## What Google evaluates (2026)

1. **Helpfulness** — does the page fully and accurately answer the query?
2. **Originality** — unique insights, real examples, depth, new angles
3. **Author trust** — visible human input, expertise signals, transparent About/Contact pages
4. **Site-level trust** — consistent quality, real brand identity, clean structure
5. **Backlinks** — quality links from other sites signal credibility
6. **Page structure** — clear H1, logical headings, short paragraphs, fast loading, clean URL, proper meta
7. **User behavior** — time on page, scroll depth, low bounce rate, continued navigation

## Media and alt text

- use images, video, tables, or quotes when they make the article more useful or clearer
- media should support content, not just fill space
- all images must include clear, natural alt text written for accessibility and clarity

## Sources and factual accuracy

- all facts, statistics, comparisons, and expert-style claims must be verified before publishing
- use reliable sources during research
- a "Sources" section may be added at the end of articles when relevant
